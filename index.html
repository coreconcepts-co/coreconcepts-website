FAQ

Answering the questions people have before they book.

Does virtual coaching actually work?
Yes — I've been offering virtual coaching for over 10 years, and doing it full-time for the past 6 years. It works because the value is in programming decisions, root cause analysis, and consistent feedback. Most people don't need someone physically spotting them; they need someone who understands why their body keeps breaking down and how to fix it. Virtual allows me to track patterns over time and adjust intelligently.
How often do we check in?
Typically weekly or bi-weekly depending on your plan, goals, and how quickly you're adapting. More frequent check-ins for athletes or complex cases, less frequent for experienced trainees with stable patterns.
Do I need a gym?
No. I can build plans around home equipment, minimal equipment, or a full gym setup. What matters is progressive overload and intelligent programming — not the specific tools.
How do I know which option is right for me?
Book a free consult. We'll clarify your goals, constraints, injury history, and match you to the best-fit option. No pressure — just a clear plan for what to do next.
What if I have old injuries or pain?
That's exactly who I work with. Most of my clients come to me with chronic issues that haven't resolved with traditional approaches. I find the root cause (often gut inflammation, nutrition, recovery, sleep, movement compensations) and address that while building strength.
What results can I expect?
Depends on your starting point and goals. Most clients see pain reduction within 2-4 weeks, movement improvements within 4-8 weeks, and measurable strength gains within 8-12 weeks. Long-term clients (12+ months) typically report being pain-free, stronger than they've been in years, and training consistently without setbacks.
